Learn Your Tennis Shots! 🎾

Every tennis superstar starts with these 4 basic shots. Learn them step-by-step in easy, fun language — let's go! 🚀

Shot 1 of 4

🤚
Forehand
⭐ Your Main Shot!

The Forehand 🤚

The forehand is the shot where you swing with your hitting hand facing the ball — like you're giving it a high-five! It's the most important shot in tennis.

  1. Get into your ready position — feet apart, knees bent.
  2. When you see the ball coming, turn your body sideways — so your hitting shoulder points at the ball.
  3. Bring your racket back — arm stretched out behind you.
  4. As the ball bounces and comes up, swing your racket low to high — like a rainbow 🌈 from your hip to your shoulder!
  5. Hit the ball when it is at hip height — in front of your body, not beside you.
  6. Finish with your racket up by your opposite shoulder. Take a bow! 🎭
🌈 Magic word: LOW TO HIGH! Always swing from low down near your hip, upward toward your shoulder. This makes the ball go over the net!
  • Swinging too flat — remember, swing LOW TO HIGH!
  • Hitting the ball too late — turn your body early!
  • Looking away from the ball — keep eyes on it!
  • Standing too close or too far — take one step away from the ball.

✋
Backhand
The Other Side!

The Backhand ✋

The backhand is for balls that come to the other side — you swing across your body! You can use one hand or two hands on the grip. Two hands is easier for beginners!

  1. Get ready! When the ball comes to your backhand side, turn your body the other way.
  2. Put your non-hitting hand on the racket too — two hands together on the handle.
  3. Bring the racket back across your body — like you're hugging yourself.
  4. As the ball bounces up, swing the racket across your body, low to high.
  5. Hit the ball out in front — don't wait for it to pass you!
  6. Finish with your racket pointing toward where the ball went — like you're pointing at a friend!
🤗 Two-hand tip: Your non-hitting hand does most of the work on a backhand — push through with it!
  • Letting the ball pass you — swing early, not late!
  • Only using one hand (at first) — two hands give more power!
  • Not turning your shoulders — really rotate your whole body.
  • Stopping the swing early — follow through all the way!

🎯
Serve
Start the Point!

The Serve 🎯

The serve starts every point — it's YOUR shot, because you get to toss the ball yourself! For red ball beginners, we use an easy underarm serve that's simple and fun to learn.

  1. Stand behind the baseline (the back line). Hold the ball in your non-hitting hand.
  2. For a beginner serve, hold the ball at waist height and swing underarm — like a bowling toss!
  3. Toss the ball slightly forward and at the same time swing your racket forward and UP.
  4. Hit the ball when it's in front of you — aim it diagonally across the net into the service box.
  5. Follow through — your racket should end up pointing where the ball went.
  6. Bonus: For overarm serve, toss the ball up above your head and swing DOWN onto it — like scratching the sky! 🌤️
🎳 Beginner trick: Think of the underarm serve like a slow-motion bowling throw — toss forward, swing forward!
  • Tossing too far behind you — toss just slightly in front!
  • Swinging too hard — a slow, smooth serve is better than a fast wild one.
  • Forgetting to aim — pick a spot to aim at before you serve.
  • Faulting (missing the service box) — aim higher over the net.

👊
Volley
At the Net!

The Volley 👊

A volley is when you hit the ball in the air before it bounces! You do this up at the net — quick short punches, like you're blocking in slow motion. No big swing!

  1. Move up to the net — stand about 2 steps from it.
  2. Get in your ready position — racket up in front of you!
  3. When the ball comes, make a short punching motion forward — no big backswing!
  4. Keep your wrist firm and stiff — don't let it flop around.
  5. Make contact in front of your body — never let it reach your side.
  6. Just a small follow-through — imagine you're gently pushing the ball away.
🥊 The golden rule: NO BIG SWING! A volley is a small, controlled PUNCH. The ball already has speed — you just guide it!
  • Taking a big swing — keep it short and punchy!
  • Letting the ball get beside you — meet it in FRONT.
  • Dropping the racket head — keep the racket face up and firm.
  • Running away from the net — stay close, stay brave! 💪

🌟 Did You Know? 🌟

Professional tennis players practice the same basic shots — forehand, backhand, serve, volley — every single day. The difference between a beginner and a pro is just how many times they've practiced! Keep hitting! 🏅
