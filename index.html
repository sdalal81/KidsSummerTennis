Why Red Ball Tennis? 🔴

Red balls bounce slower and lower — perfect for little players learning big skills!

🐢

Slower Ball = More Fun

Red foam balls travel 75% slower than regular balls. You have more time to see it, get ready, and swing!

📏

Smaller Court

We play on a mini court that's just the right size for young players. No running too far!

🏸

Lighter Racket

Junior rackets are shorter and lighter so little arms can swing easily without getting tired.

😊

More Rallies!

Because the ball is slower, you can keep it going back and forth — that means more fun rallies!

🧠

Build Real Skills

You learn the same real tennis techniques — forehand, backhand, serve, volley — just made easier!

🌍

Used Worldwide

Tennis 10s and red ball programs are used by tennis clubs in 180+ countries around the world!

Why pay club fees before your child even knows they love tennis?

Club memberships, court bookings, coaching packages — the costs add up fast. Start here first. Build the basics at your local park, see if your child takes to it, and then decide if a club is worth it. No commitment, no contracts — just fun tennis at a price that makes sense.

Get Your Basic Skills First 🚀

Five simple steps and your child is on the court!

🏸

Find a Racket

Use a small junior racket (17–21 inches). Second-hand ones work great!

👕

Dress Comfortable

Wear loose, easy-to-move-in clothes and trainers with good grip — no special kit needed!

💧

Grab a Drink

Bring a water bottle to stay hydrated — you'll be running around and working up a sweat!

🌳

Head to the Park

We play at Tom Sawyer Park and Briar Hill Park — no booking needed!

🧑

Bring a Grown-Up

Have a parent or adult nearby — they can cheer, feed balls, or just watch!

🏅

Play & Have Fun!

Hit rallies, play games, keep it casual — every swing counts!
