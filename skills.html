First: The Ready Position! 🦸

Before every shot, get into your ready position — this is how all tennis players stand!

🧍
Ready Position!

Stand Like a Tennis Superhero! 🦸‍♂️

  • 👟 Feet shoulder-width apart — like you're standing on two trampolines!
  • 🦵 Bend your knees slightly — stay bouncy and ready to move!
  • 🏸 Hold your racket up in front of you with both hands — one on the grip, one touching the neck.
  • 👀 Eyes forward, watching the ball — like a hawk! 🦅
  • 💃 Stay on your toes, rocking gently — never stand still!
💡 Coach Tip: Practice your ready position every morning for 30 seconds — it will become automatic!

The 5 Super Drills 🎯

Try each drill and get your star when you can do it 5 times in a row!

1
🎈

Balloon Taps

Tap a balloon up in the air and keep it from touching the ground! This is the best drill for beginners because the balloon is huge and slow — perfect for learning to watch and swing!

How to do it:
  • Blow up a regular party balloon
  • Hold your racket in your hitting hand
  • Tap the balloon upward with your racket face
  • Try to tap it 5 times without dropping it!
  • Challenge: Can you tap it 10 times? 20?
🌟 Star Challenge: 10 taps in a row!
⭐ Easy — great for beginners!
2
🔴

Bounce & Catch

Bounce a red foam ball on the ground and catch it with your hands! This teaches you to watch the ball and judge where it will go — the most important skill in tennis!

How to do it:
  • Hold the red ball in one hand
  • Drop it to the ground — let it bounce up
  • Catch it with two hands as it comes up
  • Try bouncing and catching with one hand!
  • Next: bounce, clap once, then catch!
👀 Key skill: Keep your eyes on the ball the whole time — watch it all the way into your hands!
⭐ Easy — great for beginners!
3
⚖️

Racket Balancing

Balance the red ball on your racket face and walk around without it falling off! This builds a gentle touch and teaches you to feel the racket — just like a magician!

How to do it:
  • Place the red ball on the flat face of your racket
  • Hold the racket steady — don't let the ball roll off!
  • Take 3 steps forward without dropping it
  • Try turning around in a full circle!
  • Advanced: walk backwards with the ball on the racket
🎩 Magic tip: Look at the ball, not at your feet — that's how you keep it balanced!
⭐⭐ Medium — takes practice!
4
🧱

Wall Rally

Hit the red ball against a wall and keep the rally going! The wall never misses — it will always hit it back to you, making it the perfect practice partner!

How to do it:
  • Stand about 2–3 big steps from a wall
  • Drop the ball, let it bounce, then hit it toward the wall
  • When it bounces back, hit it again!
  • Count how many times in a row you can hit it
  • Try moving back one step each time you get to 5!
🧱 Pro secret: Aim at the same spot on the wall every time — that gives you a steady bounce back!
⭐⭐ Medium — takes practice!
5
🎯

Target Toss

Set up hoops, chalk circles, or buckets on the ground and aim your shots at them! Hitting a target teaches you control and accuracy — the secret weapon of great tennis players!

How to do it:
  • Place a hoop or draw a circle on the ground (2 metres away)
  • Drop the ball, bounce it, and try to hit it into the target
  • Score 1 point for each ball that lands in!
  • Move the target farther away for more challenge
  • Try 3 different targets and aim for each one
🎯 Aim trick: Look at the target BEFORE you swing — then your body knows where to send the ball!
⭐⭐⭐ Tricky — you've got this!
6
👫

Partner Rally

Hit the ball back and forth with a friend, parent, or coach! Rallying together is the heart of tennis — work as a team to keep the ball going as long as possible!

How to do it:
  • Stand about 3–4 big steps apart from your partner
  • One person drops and hits the ball to the other
  • The other person lets it bounce once, then hits it back
  • Count every hit together — that's your rally score!
  • Celebrate when you beat your record! 🎉
🤝 Team tip: Hit the ball gently to your partner — not too hard, not too soft. Help each other succeed!
⭐⭐⭐ Tricky — you've got this!
