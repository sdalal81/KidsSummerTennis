What Is Red Ball Tennis?

Red ball tennis (also called Tennis 10s or Mini Tennis) is a special version of tennis designed just for children aged 5–10. Instead of using fast, hard regular tennis balls, players use soft red foam balls that bounce more slowly and at a lower height — making the game much easier and more fun for young learners!

The program was created by the International Tennis Federation (ITF) and is now used in over 180 countries worldwide. Best of all, you don't need a club or coach to get started — a public park court, a cheap junior racket, a few foam balls, and a responsible adult nearby is all you need. Keep it casual, keep it fun!

As you get better, you move to faster balls! Each colour is a new level!

50% slower 🚶

Why Red Ball Is Amazing for Kids! 🌟

Here's why every child aged 5–10 should try red ball tennis!

🧠

Builds Brain Power

Tennis improves focus, concentration, and quick thinking as you track the ball and plan your shots.

👁️

Hand-Eye Coordination

Hitting a moving ball develops hand-eye coordination faster than almost any other sport!

💪

Gets You Moving

Running, jumping, reaching, and swinging gives your whole body a great workout — it's fun exercise!

😊

Builds Confidence

Every time you make a great shot, your confidence grows. Tennis teaches you to celebrate small wins!

🤝

Social Skills

Playing with a partner teaches teamwork, sportsmanship, and how to win and lose gracefully.

📈

Clear Progression

Red → Orange → Green → Yellow! Kids can see their progress clearly as they level up!

🧑

Safe & Supervised

Sessions at public courts and parks with an adult nearby keep things safe, relaxed, and enjoyable for everyone.

🦴

Healthy Bones & Muscles

Playing tennis regularly helps build strong bones and muscles during the key growing years.

🎯

Real Tennis Skills

The techniques kids learn with red balls are exactly the same as pro tennis — just slowed down!

What Equipment Do You Need? 🛍️

You don't need much to get started — here's the kit list!

🎾

Red Foam Ball

A soft, squishy foam ball — a few dollars online or at sports stores. Much easier to hit than a regular ball!

🏸

Junior Racket

A 17–21 inch junior racket. Second-hand ones from charity shops or Facebook Marketplace are totally fine!

🌳

Public Court or Park

We play at Tom Sawyer Park and Briar Hill Park. No need to book a club — just show up!

🧑

A Supervising Adult

A parent, carer, or responsible adult nearby — they don't need to play, just be around to keep things safe and fun.

👟

Sport Shoes

Any trainers with a bit of grip. Full tennis shoes are nice but definitely not required!

💧

Water Bottle

Always bring water! Tennis is hard work and you need to stay hydrated to play your best. 🥤

😃

A Big Smile!

The most important piece of equipment! This is casual, relaxed tennis — just come and enjoy it!

How Red Ball Tennis Came to Be 📖

A short story of how mini tennis was invented!

🌱 The Problem (1990s)

Tennis coaches noticed that young children were struggling with regular tennis. The ball was too fast, the court too big, and the racket too heavy. Many kids gave up quickly because it felt too hard.

💡 The Idea

The International Tennis Federation (ITF) came up with a plan: create a graded progression system using different coloured balls of different speeds. Start slow, get faster over time!

🚀 Tennis 10s Launched

The ITF officially launched Tennis 10s — a complete program for children aged 10 and under with red, orange, and green balls and smaller courts and rackets.

🌍 Worldwide Success

Today, red ball programs are run in 180+ countries. Millions of children learn tennis this way every year, including future champions! Your journey starts now! 🏆
